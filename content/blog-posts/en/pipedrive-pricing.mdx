# Pipedrive Pricing 2026: Every Plan, Hidden Cost, and Cheaper Alternatives

Pipedrive plans range from $14 to $99 per user per month, but add-ons and tier upgrades push real costs much higher. Here is what you will actually pay and when a cheaper CRM makes more sense.

Pipedrive is one of the most popular CRM platforms for sales teams. Its visual pipeline interface and activity-based selling approach make it genuinely useful for salespeople who need to manage deals without wrestling with complexity. But the sticker price on the pricing page tells only part of the story.

Once you factor in add-ons like LeadBooster ($32.50/month), Web Visitors ($41-$299/month), Campaigns ($13.33/month), and the feature limitations that push teams into higher tiers, the real cost of Pipedrive is often 50 to 100 percent more than the base plan price suggests.

This guide breaks down every Pipedrive plan as it stands in 2026, explains every hidden cost, compares Pipedrive against alternatives, and helps you decide whether Pipedrive is worth the investment. If you are comparing [CRM costs](/blog/crm-cost) across vendors, this is the most complete Pipedrive pricing analysis available.

## How Much Does Pipedrive Cost?

Pipedrive offers five pricing tiers. All prices are per user per month with annual billing. Monthly billing adds 20 to 30 percent.

| Plan | Annual Billing | Monthly Billing | Workflow Limit |
|---|---|---|---|
| Essential | $14/user/mo | $24/user/mo | None |
| Advanced | $34/user/mo | $44/user/mo | 30 active |
| Professional | $49/user/mo | $64/user/mo | 60 active |
| Power | $64/user/mo | $79/user/mo | 90 active |
| Enterprise | $99/user/mo | $129/user/mo | Unlimited |

For a team of 10 users on the Professional plan with annual billing, that is $490 per month or $5,880 per year in license fees alone, before any add-ons.

## Pipedrive Plans Explained

### Essential: $14 per User per Month

The entry plan for small teams getting started with pipeline management.

**Included features**:
- Contact and deal management
- Customizable sales pipelines
- Activity tracking and reminders
- Basic email inbox
- Mobile app access
- Up to 3,000 open deals

**Key limitations**:
- No full email sync (one-way only)
- No workflow automation
- No email templates or tracking
- No group emailing
- No meeting scheduler
- Basic reporting only

The Essential plan is useful for solo founders or very small teams that need nothing beyond a deal tracker. Most teams outgrow it within months.

### Advanced: $34 per User per Month

The plan where Pipedrive starts becoming a real CRM tool.

**Included features**:
- Everything in Essential
- Full two-way email sync
- Email templates and open tracking
- Workflow automation (30 active workflows)
- Group emailing
- Meeting scheduler
- Automations for repetitive tasks

**Key limitations**:
- 30 workflow automation cap
- No advanced reporting or custom dashboards
- No revenue forecasting
- No document management or e-signatures
- No phone tracking

For many small sales teams, Advanced is the minimum viable plan. The jump from $14 to $34 per user is significant, especially because email sync and basic automation are features most modern CRMs include at every tier.

### Professional: $49 per User per Month

The most popular Pipedrive plan, targeted at growing sales teams.

**Included features**:
- Everything in Advanced
- Advanced custom reports and dashboards
- Revenue forecasting and projections
- Document management with e-signatures
- 60 active automation workflows
- AI-powered sales assistant

**Key limitations**:
- No project management
- No phone tracking or call logging
- No advanced team permissions
- Still limited to 60 automation workflows

Professional is where Pipedrive delivers the most value relative to cost, but at $49 per user it is already more expensive than many full-featured alternatives.

### Power: $64 per User per Month

Designed for larger teams that need project management alongside CRM.

**Included features**:
- Everything in Professional
- Project management and tracking
- Phone tracking with call logging
- Advanced team permissions and visibility
- 90 active automation workflows

### Enterprise: $99 per User per Month

The top-tier plan with no feature restrictions.

**Included features**:
- Everything in Power
- Unlimited open deals
- Unlimited automation workflows
- Advanced security settings (SSO, 2FA enforcement)
- Dedicated implementation support
- Audit log and enhanced compliance

## The Hidden Costs of Pipedrive

The plan prices above are just the starting point. Several additional costs push the real price significantly higher.

### Add-On Costs

Pipedrive sells several capabilities as separate paid add-ons, not included in any standard plan:

| Add-On | Price | What It Does |
|---|---|---|
| LeadBooster | From $32.50/mo per company | Chatbot, live chat, web forms, prospector |
| Web Visitors | $41 to $299/mo per company | Identify companies visiting your website |
| Smart Docs | From $32.50/mo per company | Document automation and templates |
| Campaigns | From $13.33/mo per company | Email marketing campaigns |
| Projects | From $6.67/mo per user | Project management (also in Power plan) |

A team on the Professional plan that adds LeadBooster and Campaigns is spending an extra $46 per month before even counting per-user add-ons like Projects.

### Features Locked Behind Higher Tiers

Several features that competing CRMs include at all plan levels require expensive Pipedrive upgrades:

- **Full email sync**: Not available on Essential ($14). Requires Advanced ($34) minimum.
- **Any workflow automation**: Not available on Essential. Requires Advanced ($34) minimum.
- **Revenue forecasting**: Requires Professional ($49) minimum.
- **Advanced reporting**: Requires Professional ($49) minimum.
- **Phone tracking**: Requires Power ($64) minimum.
- **Unlimited automations**: Requires Enterprise ($99).

### Monthly vs. Annual Billing Surcharge

All published Pipedrive prices assume annual billing with upfront payment. Choosing monthly billing increases costs by 20 to 30 percent:

| Plan | Annual Billing | Monthly Billing | Premium |
|---|---|---|---|
| Essential | $14 | $24 | +71% |
| Advanced | $34 | $44 | +29% |
| Professional | $49 | $64 | +31% |
| Power | $64 | $79 | +23% |
| Enterprise | $99 | $129 | +30% |

The Essential plan monthly billing premium is particularly steep at 71 percent.

### Scaling Costs for Growing Teams

Pipedrive pricing scales linearly. Every new user costs the same as the first. Here is what a 20-person team pays:

| Plan | Monthly (Annual Billing) | Annually |
|---|---|---|
| Essential | $280 | $3,360 |
| Advanced | $680 | $8,160 |
| Professional | $980 | $11,760 |
| Power | $1,280 | $15,360 |
| Enterprise | $1,980 | $23,760 |

Add two add-ons and a mid-sized team on Professional can easily spend $15,000 to $20,000 per year. For context, the same 20-person team on [Customermates](/pricing) would pay $2,400 per year with all features included.

## What Pipedrive Does Well

Despite the cost concerns, Pipedrive has real strengths worth acknowledging:

- **Excellent pipeline visualization**: The drag-and-drop Kanban pipeline is one of the best visual deal trackers on the market
- **Genuinely easy to use**: Salespeople actually adopt it because the interface is intuitive and fast
- **Quick setup**: A team can be operational in hours, not weeks
- **Solid mobile app**: For field sales teams that update deals on the go
- **Activity-based selling methodology**: Focuses reps on the next action rather than getting lost in data entry
- **Large integration marketplace**: Over 400 third-party integrations available
- **14-day free trial**: No credit card required to test every feature

## Where Pipedrive Falls Short

- **Limited beyond sales**: Marketing automation, customer service, and support ticketing are either missing or rudimentary
- **No self-hosting option**: All data resides on Pipedrive's infrastructure with no on-premise alternative
- **Not open source**: No source code access, which means full vendor lock-in with no exit strategy beyond data export
- **GDPR gray areas**: While Pipedrive is headquartered in the EU (Estonia), data processing infrastructure is not guaranteed to stay within EU borders
- **Automation caps on most plans**: Workflow limits force growing teams into more expensive tiers
- **No free plan**: Unlike HubSpot or Zoho, there is no permanent free tier

## Is Pipedrive Cheaper Than HubSpot?

This is one of the most common questions when evaluating CRM pricing. The answer depends on what you need.

Pipedrive's entry price ($14/user/month) is lower than HubSpot Sales Hub Starter ($15/user/month), and significantly cheaper than HubSpot Professional ($90/user/month). However, HubSpot offers a free CRM plan with basic contact management, deal tracking, and email integration that Pipedrive does not match.

For pure sales pipeline management, Pipedrive often costs less than HubSpot at equivalent feature levels. But HubSpot includes marketing, service, and CMS tools in its platform, which means businesses needing more than sales may find HubSpot's bundled approach more cost-effective despite higher per-user prices.

Neither platform is cheap once you need advanced features. Both push critical capabilities into higher tiers and sell add-ons separately. For a detailed breakdown of CRM pricing across all major vendors, see our [CRM cost guide](/blog/crm-cost).

## Pipedrive vs. Salesforce: Which Is Better Value?

Salesforce is significantly more expensive than Pipedrive at every tier. [Salesforce Sales Cloud](/blog/salesforce-pricing) starts at $25/user/month for the Starter Suite and runs up to $500/user/month for Einstein 1 Sales. Pipedrive's Enterprise plan at $99/user/month is cheaper than Salesforce's mid-tier Professional at $80/user/month, and Salesforce requires additional spend for implementation, administration, and consulting that Pipedrive does not.

For small to mid-sized sales teams, Pipedrive delivers better value than Salesforce. Salesforce makes sense for large enterprises with complex requirements, multiple departments, and the budget for a dedicated Salesforce administrator. For everyone else, Pipedrive or a more affordable alternative like Customermates is a smarter investment.

## Can You Use Pipedrive for Free?

Pipedrive does not offer a permanent free plan. It provides a 14-day free trial that includes access to all features with no credit card required. After the trial, you must choose a paid plan or lose access.

If you need a CRM you can use for free indefinitely, consider HubSpot's free tier, Freshsales' free plan (up to 3 users), or [open-source CRM options](/blog/open-source-crm) like Customermates that offer transparent pricing starting at EUR 7 per user per month with no feature restrictions.

## When Pipedrive Is Still the Right Choice

Pipedrive can be worth the investment if:

- Your primary need is visual pipeline management and your team values simplicity above all else
- You operate with a small team (under 5 users) on the Essential or Advanced plan without needing add-ons
- Your salespeople already know and prefer Pipedrive and the switching cost outweighs the savings
- Data privacy, self-hosting, and GDPR compliance are not priorities for your organization
- You do not need marketing automation, customer service, or project management in the same platform

## When to Switch Away from Pipedrive

Consider an alternative when:

- You have outgrown the Essential plan and costs are climbing with each tier upgrade
- Workflow automation caps are limiting your team's efficiency
- You need marketing, service, or project management alongside sales CRM
- GDPR compliance and guaranteed EU data hosting are business requirements
- You want to eliminate vendor lock-in with an [open-source CRM](/blog/open-source-crm)
- Your total Pipedrive spend (plans plus add-ons) exceeds what a full-featured alternative would cost
- You are evaluating CRM options as part of a broader [CRM strategy](/blog/crm-strategy) overhaul

## Frequently Asked Questions About Pipedrive Pricing

### How much does Pipedrive cost per month?

Pipedrive costs between $14 and $99 per user per month with annual billing. The five plans are Essential ($14), Advanced ($34), Professional ($49), Power ($64), and Enterprise ($99). Monthly billing increases prices by 20 to 30 percent. Most teams need the Advanced or Professional plan, so realistic costs start at $34 per user per month.

### Can I use Pipedrive for free?

No. Pipedrive does not have a free plan. It offers a 14-day free trial with full feature access and no credit card required. After the trial, a paid subscription is mandatory. For free CRM options, see our guide to [free CRM software](/blog/free-crm).

### Is Pipedrive cheaper than HubSpot?

For pure sales CRM features, Pipedrive is generally cheaper than HubSpot Sales Hub at comparable feature levels. Pipedrive starts at $14/user/month versus HubSpot Starter at $15/user/month. However, HubSpot offers a free CRM plan that Pipedrive does not, and HubSpot bundles marketing and service tools that Pipedrive charges extra for or does not offer.

### What is better, Pipedrive or Salesforce?

Pipedrive is better for small to mid-sized sales teams that want an intuitive pipeline tool at a lower price. Salesforce is better for large enterprises with complex requirements and the budget for implementation and administration. For most businesses under 50 users, Pipedrive or an affordable alternative like [Customermates](/pricing) delivers more value per dollar than Salesforce.

### What is the cheapest Pipedrive plan?

The cheapest Pipedrive plan is Essential at $14 per user per month with annual billing ($24 with monthly billing). However, Essential lacks email sync, workflow automation, and email templates, which means most teams need the Advanced plan at $34 per user per month minimum.

### Does Pipedrive have hidden costs?

Yes. Beyond the per-user plan price, Pipedrive charges separately for add-ons including LeadBooster ($32.50/month), Web Visitors ($41-$299/month), Campaigns ($13.33/month), and Projects ($6.67/user/month). Monthly billing adds 20 to 30 percent, and critical features are locked behind higher-tier plans.
