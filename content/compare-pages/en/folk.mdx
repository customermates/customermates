> Folk CRM is a clean, relationship-first CRM that small teams pick when they have outgrown spreadsheets, Notion, or Airtable. Customermates is an open-source CRM that ships with the same modern UX but is designed to be operated by AI agents through 57 [MCP](/features/api) tools, an [n8n](/features/workflow-automation) community node, and 15 webhook events. This page compares both so you can match the right tool to your situation.

## What is Folk CRM?

Folk is a contemporary CRM built around the relationship model: contacts, deals, pipelines, and a strong LinkedIn-first import flow via a Chrome extension. The product is positioned as a simple, integrated CRM with AI assistants that draft messages, enrich contacts, and propose next actions. It is closed-source, hosted by Folk, with optional EU residency.

The Premium tier adds custom objects, email sequences, advanced permissions, and API access. Pricing is per member per month: $24 annual or $30 monthly for Standard, $48 annual or $60 monthly for Premium.

## What is Customermates?

Customermates is a CRM that updates itself if you already use [Claude](/blog/chatgpt-summarize-notes), ChatGPT, or Codex. The pipeline moves, follow-ups get drafted, and contact notes stay current because the agent does the typing. Underneath that outcome is a modern Pipedrive replacement: contacts, organizations, deals, services, tasks, two views (table and Kanban), eight custom field types, and a full REST API. Open-source under AGPL-3.0, free to self-host via Docker, EU-hosted in the cloud from €9 per user per month on the yearly plan.

The mechanism that makes the AI integration real is a built-in MCP server with 57 tools, an n8n community node, 15 webhook events, and the OpenAPI 3.1 specification at `/api/v1/openapi`.

## Setup and onboarding

Both products are quick to set up compared to legacy CRMs. Folk's strength is the LinkedIn Chrome extension, which makes contact import painless when LinkedIn is your sourcing channel. The default views and templates work for relationship-driven sales out of the box.

Customermates is also ready in two minutes. The Card view groups by any single-select column, so any entity (deals, contacts, organizations, tasks) can be a Kanban board. There is no native LinkedIn extension, but the MCP server plus an Apify or n8n flow covers the same import pattern programmatically.

## CRM depth

Folk's CRM is competent for the relationship-first use case. Contacts, deals, pipelines, simple custom fields on Standard. Custom objects unlock on Premium. Email sequences are native. The AI assistants draft messages and enrich profiles using credits (500 enrichments per month on Standard, 1,000 on Premium).

Customermates ships with five entity types as first-class citizens: contacts, organizations, deals, services, tasks. All link bidirectionally via a single `link_entities` operation. Custom fields are unlimited and typed (eight types: plain, date, datetime, currency, single-select, link, email, phone), available from day one rather than as an upsell. The REST API and MCP server expose every operation, which means email sequences, enrichment, and follow-up drafting happen via your own n8n flows or Claude prompts at no extra cost.

## AI: assistants vs. an agent that runs the CRM

Folk's AI is built into the UI: assistants draft messages, summarize threads, and propose enrichments. You stay in the CRM and the AI suggests. It is convenient and good at what it does.

Customermates was designed so AI agents operate the CRM directly. The MCP server exposes 57 tools that map to every CRM operation: create deals, update notes, link entities, configure custom columns, run filters, list webhooks. Point Claude or Codex at `/api/v1/mcp` with your API key and the model can read and write the CRM as if it were a human user. The same plumbing powers a [native n8n community node](/blog/n8n-for-beginners-build-your-first-workflow) and 15 webhook events, so AI workflows in n8n stay synchronized without polling.

The difference matters when you want the CRM to update itself based on email, calls, transcripts, or chat instead of nudging a human to copy-paste. Folk's AI helps you type less. Customermates' AI lets you stop typing.

## Open-source and data sovereignty

Folk is closed-source and hosted by Folk. EU residency is offered. Migration off Folk means an export and a rebuild on the next CRM.

Customermates is licensed under [AGPL-3.0](https://www.gnu.org/licenses/agpl-3.0.html). The full source is on [GitHub](https://github.com/customermates/customermates). Self-host via `docker compose up -d`, which runs three containers (app, PostgreSQL 16, webhook worker). The cloud version is hosted in Germany, GDPR-native by default. If the project ever stops, your data and the code are still yours.

## When Folk CRM is the better choice

If your sourcing channel is LinkedIn-first and you want a polished native Chrome extension to capture profiles in one click, Folk is hard to beat. The product is well-made, the UI is friendly, and the AI assistants for drafting and enrichment are mature. It is a strong choice for solo founders or small teams who value the LinkedIn-native flow over agent-driven automation, and who are happy with a closed-source SaaS.

## When Customermates is the better choice

If you already use Claude, ChatGPT, or Codex and want an AI agent to actually operate the CRM (not just suggest things in a sidebar), Customermates was built for that. If you want open-source, self-hostable, EU-hosted, AGPL-licensed CRM with no vendor lock-in, Folk does not offer it. If you want to pay €9 per user instead of $24 to $48 per member and get full API, MCP, n8n integration, and webhooks from day one without a Premium upgrade, the math is clear.

## Summary

Folk CRM is a polished relationship-first CRM with strong LinkedIn ergonomics and helpful AI assistants. Customermates is an open-source CRM with the same modern UX but designed from the ground up so AI agents can operate it directly through 57 MCP tools, n8n, and webhooks. Both work for small teams. Pick Folk when LinkedIn is your channel and you want a closed-source SaaS that helps you type less. Pick Customermates when you want the AI to type for you, and when open-source, EU hosting, and self-hosting are part of "done."

## Frequently asked questions

**Is folk CRM good?**
Folk is a well-built CRM for solo founders and small teams that source through LinkedIn. The UI is clean, the Chrome extension makes contact import painless, and the AI assistants for drafting and enrichment are mature. It is a strong choice when relationship management is the core workflow. The main constraint is per-member pricing that climbs fast with team size, no self-hosting option, and AI that suggests rather than operates the CRM directly.

**Is folk CRM free?**
No. Folk has a 14-day free trial of the Premium tier on signup. After that the Standard plan is $24 per member per month annually or $30 monthly, and Premium is $48 annually or $60 monthly. Customermates offers a 7-day Pro trial without a credit card and is also free to self-host under AGPL-3.0.

**Who owns Folk CRM?**
Folk is built by a Paris-based startup founded by Simo Lemhandez and team. It is a closed-source SaaS company hosted on third-party infrastructure, with optional EU residency for European customers.

**Does folk integrate with LinkedIn?**
Yes, via a native Chrome extension that captures profiles into the CRM with one click. This is one of the strongest LinkedIn-first CRM flows available. Customermates does not have a native LinkedIn extension but covers the same workflow programmatically through the MCP server, REST API, and n8n / Apify scrapers.

**What is the best alternative to folk CRM?**
Depends on the use case. For LinkedIn-first relationship CRM with built-in AI assistants, [Attio](https://attio.com) and [Dex](https://getdex.com) are direct competitors. For an open-source, agent-native CRM that is cheaper per user and ships full API + MCP + webhooks at the base tier, Customermates is the closest match. For broader CRM alternatives see the [Pipedrive alternative](/compare/pipedrive-alternative) and [HubSpot alternative](/compare/hubspot-alternative) pages.
